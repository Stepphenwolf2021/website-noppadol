The World's Manufacturing Powerhouse

In 2025 China's combined goods trade reached a record US$6.36 trillion. Exports outpaced a modest rise in imports, pushing the annual goods-trade surplus past US$1 trillion for the first time. December alone set a single-month record of US$617 billion.

    A Decade of Trade Growth

    From roughly US$3.7 trillion in total goods trade in 2016 to a record US$6.36 trillion in 2025 — driven by the post-pandemic export surge and the rise of advanced, high-value manufacturing.

    Who China Trades With — and What It Sells

    In 2025 ASEAN overtook all others as China's largest trading partner, the EU ranked second, and exports to the United States fell sharply amid tariffs. Electrical machinery dominates the export mix; energy and chips dominate imports.

        👜 Personal Luxury Goods Market

        After a steep 2024 (−17% to −19%), mainland China's personal luxury market softened more gently in 2025 (−3% to −5%) to about US$46 billion. Leather goods dominate; watches remain under pressure. Women account for ~54% of luxury buyers.
