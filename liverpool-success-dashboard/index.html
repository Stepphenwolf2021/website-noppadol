Milestones and Legends (1892 - 2026)

Chronological milestones detailing the greatest matches, legendary managers, and massive achievements of the club.

June 1892

Founding of Liverpool F.C.

Following a dispute over rent at Anfield stadium between the Everton board and John Houlding, Everton relocated to Goodison Park. Left with an empty stadium, Houlding founded Liverpool Football Club. In their inaugural 1892-93 season, they won the Lancashire League title unbeaten.

Club Founded
May 1901

First League Title

Managed by Tom Watson, Liverpool captured their first-ever First Division League Championship. They won 19 games and secured 45 points, finishing two points clear of runners-up Sunderland, establishing themselves as a force in English football.

League Title
May 1965

Bill Shankly Wins First FA Cup

After joining the club in 1959 and gaining promotion from the Second Division in 1962, Bill Shankly guided Liverpool to their first-ever FA Cup trophy in club history, defeating Leeds United 2-1 after extra time at Wembley Stadium.

FA Cup Title
May 1977

Rome & The First European Cup

Under Bob Paisley, Liverpool captured their first-ever European Cup (UEFA Champions League) title by defeating Borussia Mönchengladbach 3-1 in a legendary final in Rome, kickstarting an era of dominant continental conquest.

European Champions
May 1984

Joe Fagan's Historic Treble

Joe Fagan's side recorded an unprecedented treble by winning the First Division League Title, beating Everton to lift the League Cup, and defeating AS Roma in their own stadium on penalties to claim Liverpool's fourth European Cup.

Historic Treble
May 1986

The Player-Manager Double

In his first season as player-manager, Kenny Dalglish led the club to their first domestic League & FA Cup double. Dalglish scored the title-winning league goal against Chelsea, and Liverpool beat Everton 3-1 in the FA Cup final.

Domestic Double
May 2001

The Historic Cup Treble

Under Gérard Houllier, Liverpool achieved a unique cup treble. They won the League Cup, staged a late Michael Owen comeback to beat Arsenal 2-1 in the FA Cup, and conquered Alavés 5-4 in a wild UEFA Cup Final ending in a golden goal.

3 Trophies
May 2005

The Miracle of Istanbul

Trailing AC Milan 3-0 at half-time in the UEFA Champions League Final, Liverpool staged the greatest comeback in football history. Goals from Steven Gerrard, Vladimír Šmicer, and Xabi Alonso drew them level, winning on penalties to secure their 5th European Cup.

Champions League
May 2019

4-0 Barcelona Miracle & 6th Champions League

Trailing Barcelona 3-0 in the UCL semi-final, Liverpool blew Barca away 4-0 at Anfield, highlighted by Trent's quick corner. Liverpool went on to defeat Tottenham 2-0 in Madrid to secure their 6th European Cup under Jürgen Klopp.

Champions League
June 2020

The 30-Year Premier League Wait Ends

In a record-shattering campaign, Liverpool clinched their first Premier League title, winning the league with an unprecedented 7 games remaining. Klopp's team accumulated 99 points, recording 32 wins, 3 draws, and only 3 defeats.

Premier League
May 2025

Arne Slot clinches Premier League Title

Continuing the legacy, Dutch coach Arne Slot led Liverpool to a magnificent league victory in his first season. Winning 25 matches and finishing on 84 points, they clinched the trophy with four games to spare, showcasing the club's incredible structural stability.

Premier League
June 2026

Andoni Iraola Appointed Head Coach

Following Arne Slot's tenure, Basque tactician Andoni Iraola was appointed to lead Liverpool F.C., bringing his signature high-pressing, intense tactical style from Bournemouth to Anfield.
